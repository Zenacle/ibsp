Water Quiz
Before You

A city of 12 million ran out of water. Now, test what you know.
Chennai — June 2019
How a city of 12 million ran out of water.
In 1970, Chennai had 3.2 million people. Groundwater was accessible at 5–10m. By 2019, all 20 groundwater units were officially 'over-exploited'. Four major reservoirs — Poondi, Cholavaram, Red Hills, Chembarambakkam — hit 0.1% capacity.
80% groundwater depleted 0.1% reservoir capacity
The infrastructure built on drained wetlands was flying water in on trucks.
Quick question
How much water does one person need per day — India's national standard?
A
160 litres
B
200 litres
C
50 litres
D
135 litres
D — 135 litres (BIS Standard)
The WHO minimum is just 50L for basic health and dignity. India's BIS standard is 135L. But urban India's actual usage? 135–200 litres per person per day — much of it wasted.
50L WHO minimum 135L BIS standard 135–200L urban actual
